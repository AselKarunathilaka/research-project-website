About WILDGuard

A scalable, IoT solution for crop protection

Monkey depredation causes massive losses in Sri Lankan coconut plantations. WILDGuard is built to reduce crop damage by automatically detecting monkey presence and activating humane deterrents — all through a low-cost, battery-efficient IoT network.

Designed for rural deployment, the system uses motion, ultrasonic and thermal sensing combined with edge analytics to discriminate monkeys from other animals and false alarms. When a confirmed detection occurs, WILDGuard triggers a staged deterrence response (ultrasound, flashing lights, laser pulses) and logs activity for farmer review.

Project Components

Subsystems that work together in the field

Detection Subsystem

Edge detection and sensor fusion to reliably identify monkeys in the plantation.

  • Multi Class Model
  • YOLOV8s Model for detection
  • Multiframe classification to reduce false positives
  • Low-power sampling for long battery life

Deterrence Subsystem

Multi-modal, humane deterrents automatically triggered upon confirmed detection.

  • Ultrasonic sound emitters
  • Flashing LED arrays / strobes
  • Low-power laser pulses (non-harmful)
  • Staged escalation to minimize disturbance

IoT Communication

Reliable long-range communication tailored for remote farms.

  • LoRa for long-range, low-power comms
  • Custom Multi-hop routing & retry mechanisms
  • ESP32-based gateway & Relay node
  • Fault-Tolarant Data transmission Design

Monitoring & Optimization

Dashboarding and analytics to refine deterrence strategies and measure impact.

  • Real-time activity heatmaps
  • Deterrent performance metrics
  • Predictive Analysis
  • Data-driven optimization of thresholds

01 Literature Review

Coconut farming in Sri Lanka has been severely impacted by wildlife, particularly monkeys such as the toque macaque. Studies show that nearly 7% of the annual coconut yield is lost due to animal interference, resulting in economic losses exceeding USD 19 million annually. Previous research highlights that these animals act out of survival instincts rather than intentional destruction, making humane deterrence crucial.

Recent IoT-based approaches, including smart surveillance, thermal imaging, and machine learning-based animal classification, have shown promise. However, existing systems often suffer from high cost, limited rural connectivity, and poor real-time performance. Research by Muthumani (2023) and A.S.S et al. (2024) demonstrates the feasibility of IoT-based animal detection but emphasizes the need for low-power, scalable, and sustainable solutions adaptable to Sri Lanka's agricultural landscape.

02 Research Problem

Coconut plantations across 21 districts face continuous destruction caused by monkeys, leading to reduced yield and financial instability among rural farmers. Despite attempts using traditional deterrents (e.g., scarecrows, firecrackers), these methods are ineffective, inconsistent, and sometimes harmful.

There is a lack of an automated, eco-friendly, and affordable system capable of detecting monkey presence in real time and responding with humane deterrent mechanisms. Therefore, the problem addressed by this research is:

“How can an IoT-based real-time system detect and deter monkeys in coconut plantations effectively while ensuring safety, sustainability, and affordability for local farmers?”

03 Objectives

3.1 Main Objective

  • Design and deploy WILDGuard — an IoT-based detection and deterrence system to reduce monkey-related damage in coconut plantations while remaining humane and cost-effective.

3.2 Sub-Objectives

  • Implement multimodal detection using motion, ultrasonic, and thermal sensors.
  • Develop edge classification algorithms to minimize false positives.
  • Design a staged, humane deterrence actuator suite (ultrasound, lights, lasers).
  • Implement resilient LoRaWAN communication with multi-hop and retry mechanisms.
  • Provide farmer dashboards with heatmaps and actionable metrics.

04 Methodology

  • Requirement Analysis: Study monkey behavior, environmental conditions, and farmer needs.
  • System Design:
    • Hardware: PIR sensors, ultrasonic sensors, thermal cameras, LoRa modules.
    • Software: YOLO-based detection, edge classification, dashboard visualization.
  • Prototype Development: Integrate detection, deterrent, communication, and power subsystems.
  • Testing: Field trials in coconut farms to evaluate detection accuracy, deterrent success rate, and energy efficiency.
  • Evaluation: Collect quantitative performance data and qualitative farmer feedback for continuous system optimization.

05 Research Gap

While previous studies introduced IoT-based animal detection systems, most lacked comprehensive deterrence, real-time responsiveness, or low-power communication suitable for rural areas. Few solutions combine machine learning, LoRaWAN, and renewable energy into a single framework.

The WILDGuard project bridges this gap by delivering an integrated, humane, and data-driven system that empowers farmers through accessible technology and real-time insights.
